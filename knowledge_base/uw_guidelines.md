# Property Underwriting Guidelines — UK Personal Lines
## Version 2.1 | Effective 1 January 2026

---

## 1. Scope and Purpose

These guidelines apply to all UK personal lines property submissions including
buildings insurance, contents insurance, and combined policies. Underwriters
must apply these rules in conjunction with current rate tables and individual
risk judgement. Where a submission falls outside these guidelines, it must be
referred to a senior underwriter.

---

## 2. Acceptable Risk Criteria

### 2.1 Property Type
The following property types are within standard appetite:
- Detached, semi-detached, and terraced houses
- Purpose-built flats (maximum 6 storeys)
- Converted flats in buildings up to 4 storeys
- Bungalows

The following require referral:
- Commercial premises used partly for residential purposes
- Properties used for business activities (other than home office)
- Properties with more than one kitchen (potential HMO)
- Listed buildings (Grade I or II)
- Thatched roof properties

The following are outside appetite and must be declined:
- Unoccupied properties vacant for more than 60 consecutive days
- Properties under active renovation or structural repair
- Mobile homes and park homes

### 2.2 Construction
Standard accepted construction materials:
- Brick or stone walls with slate or tile roof: standard rate
- Concrete construction: standard rate
- Cavity wall insulation present: preferred rate (5% discount)

Referral required:
- Timber frame construction: refer to senior underwriter
- Properties with flat roofs covering more than 25% of total roof area
- Non-standard wall materials (e.g. cob, adobe)

Decline:
- Timber frame construction combined with Zone 3a or Zone 3b flood risk
- Properties where structural survey has identified active subsidence

### 2.3 Year of Construction
- Post-1945: standard appetite
- 1919–1945: standard appetite, check for subsidence history
- Pre-1919: acceptable with survey confirmation of roof condition
- Pre-1800: refer to senior underwriter
- Pre-1700: outside appetite, decline

## 3. Flood Risk Guidelines

### 3.1 Environment Agency Flood Zone Classification
All properties must be assessed against the Environment Agency flood zone map.

**Zone 1 (Low probability — <0.1% annual chance):**
- Standard appetite
- No premium loading required

**Zone 2 (Medium probability — 0.1%–1% annual chance):**
- Within appetite
- Apply 15% premium loading on buildings element
- Flood excess: minimum £500

**Zone 3a (High probability — >1% annual chance):**
- Refer to senior underwriter
- If accepted: apply 35% premium loading on buildings element
- Flood excess: minimum £2,500
- Flood Re cession must be considered

**Zone 3b (Functional floodplain):**
- Outside standard appetite
- Decline unless Flood Re cession applies and property has flood resilience measures
- If Flood Re applies: refer to Flood Re team for pricing

### 3.2 Flood Re Eligibility
Flood Re is a government-backed reinsurance scheme for high flood-risk properties.

Properties eligible for Flood Re cession:
- Built before 1 January 2009
- Used as the policyholder's primary residence
- Council tax band A through G (Band H excluded)
- Not a small business property

If a Zone 3a or 3b property is Flood Re eligible, refer to the Flood Re team
rather than declining outright. The Flood Re team will provide a cession price
based on council tax band.

### 3.3 Surface Water Flood Risk
In addition to river flood zone, assess surface water flood depth:
- Depth < 0.15m at 1-in-75 year return period: no additional loading
- Depth 0.15m–0.50m: additional 10% loading
- Depth > 0.50m: refer to senior underwriter

## 4. Claims History Guidelines

### 4.1 Standard Criteria
Claims history over the previous 5 years is a mandatory underwriting input.

| Claims in 5 years | Action |
|---|---|
| 0 | Standard rate |
| 1 | Standard rate, note claim type |
| 2 | 20% premium loading |
| 3 | Refer to senior underwriter |
| 4 or more | Decline |

### 4.2 Claim Type Adjustments
Certain claim types carry additional loading regardless of frequency:

- **Subsidence**: refer on first occurrence; decline on second
- **Escape of water (>£10,000)**: 15% additional loading
- **Flood**: apply cumulative flood loading; refer if 2+ flood claims
- **Arson/malicious damage**: refer to senior underwriter; consider fraud referral
- **Theft (3+ incidents)**: refer; assess security measures

### 4.3 Claims Anomaly
Where the applicant's declared claims count does not match verified industry
records by more than 1 claim, the submission must be referred to the
compliance team for material misrepresentation review before underwriting
can proceed.

## 5. Sum Insured Guidelines

### 5.1 Buildings Sum Insured
- Minimum acceptable: £75,000
- Maximum without referral: £1,000,000
- £1,000,001 – £2,500,000: refer to senior underwriter
- Above £2,500,000: refer to high-value property team
- Above £5,000,000: outside standard appetite

Rebuilding cost must be assessed at current RICS rebuild cost rates.
Under-insurance of more than 20% against RICS rebuild cost triggers referral.

### 5.2 Contents Sum Insured
- Maximum without referral: £100,000
- £100,001 – £250,000: refer to senior underwriter
- Above £250,000: refer to high-value property team

## 6. Crime Risk Guidelines

Crime risk is assessed using the ONS-aligned property crime index (0–100).

| Crime band | Crime index | Action |
|---|---|---|
| LOW | 0–30 | Standard rate |
| MEDIUM | 31–60 | Standard rate; ensure standard security |
| HIGH | 61–80 | 10% premium loading; minimum security requirements apply |
| VERY HIGH | 81–100 | Refer to senior underwriter; enhanced security survey required |

Minimum security requirements for HIGH crime band:
- Five-lever mortise deadlock on all external doors (BS3621)
- Window locks on all accessible windows
- Alarm system (minimum Grade 2, ARC-monitored)

---

## 7. Mandatory Referral Triggers

Any of the following automatically requires referral to a senior underwriter:

1. Timber frame construction (any flood zone)
2. Zone 3a flood risk
3. Surface water flood depth > 0.50m at 1-in-75 years
4. 3 or more claims in the past 5 years
5. Any subsidence claim in the past 5 years
6. Claims anomaly detected (declared vs verified discrepancy > 1)
7. Sum insured above £1,000,000 (buildings) or £100,000 (contents)
8. Property built before 1800
9. Very high crime band (index > 80)
10. Outstanding claims at time of submission
11. Property partly used for business purposes
12. Listed building

---

## 8. Mandatory Decline Criteria

The following constitute grounds for automatic decline:

1. Zone 3b flood risk (unless Flood Re cession applies)
2. Timber frame construction AND Zone 3a or 3b flood risk
3. 4 or more claims in the past 5 years
4. Two or more subsidence claims
5. Property built before 1700
6. Unoccupied property (vacant > 60 days)
7. Property under active structural renovation
8. Sum insured above £5,000,000
9. Active structural survey showing subsidence risk
10. Material misrepresentation confirmed by compliance team

## 9. Premium Loading Reference

| Risk factor | Loading |
|---|---|
| Zone 2 flood | +15% on buildings |
| Zone 3a flood | +35% on buildings |
| Surface water 0.15–0.50m | +10% |
| 2 claims in 5 years | +20% |
| Timber frame (if accepted) | +25% |
| High crime band | +10% |
| Pre-1919 construction | +5% |
| Escape of water >£10k claim | +15% |
| Outstanding claims (if accepted) | +30% |

Loadings are cumulative. Total loading above 60% requires senior underwriter
sign-off regardless of other criteria.
